ʻO koʻu ʻohana kēia (minus my dad taking the photo), and we were visiting Maui. An interesting thing to note is that e komo mai is typically used to say welcome. However, they decided to use e kipa mai, which also means welcome, but implies that you are only visiting. E komo mai should be used when welcoming someone to stay (for example at your home). In this case it could mean, you're only welcome to stay for a little while!
